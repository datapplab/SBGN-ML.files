Check what happened to the most narrowly distributed pathway “mineralocorticoid biosynthesis: MetaCyc PWY66-382”

It looks like this pathway has only two KOs. One of the KO (K07433) is missing in a lot of species.

read.table("http://rest.kegg.jp/link/genes/K07433")
##          V1            V2
## 1 ko:K07433      hsa:1585
## 2 ko:K07433 pps:100971238
## 3 ko:K07433    mcc:695914
## 4 ko:K07433 mcf:102137301
## 5 ko:K07433    mmu:110115
## 6 ko:K07433    rno:500892

K07433 was only detected in 6 species, according to KEGG ortholog definition. This is why the pathway is predicted to exist in only 5 species( the other KO:K00513 is missing in pps, so it is 5=6-1)

KEGG doesn’t have this pathway. In MetaCyc, it is annotated to exist in Mammalia: https://biocyc.org/META/NEW-IMAGE?type=PATHWAY&object=PWY66-382 In reactome, this pathway is annotated in many other species, as distant as Saccharomyces cerevisiae https://reactome.org/content/detail/R-HSA-193993 It doesn’t make much sense that S.cerevisiae has this pathway because “Mineralocorticoids are a class of corticosteroids, which in turn are a class of steroid hormones. Mineralocorticoids are produced in the adrenal cortex and influence salt and water balances.”

So species-specific pathway prediction is affected by ortholog prediction: KEGG’s BlastKOALA only uses sequence similarity but reactome’s Compara fits similarity network of proteins to species tree. It looks like MetaCyc’s species distribution (Mammalia) makes more sense.

To solve this issue, maybe I can try different ortholog annotation databases/algorithms
